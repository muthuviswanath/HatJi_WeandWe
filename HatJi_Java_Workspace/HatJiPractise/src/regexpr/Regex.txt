The patterns can be represented using character classes

[abcd]		-	This means that the pattern can be  containing a or b or c or d
[^abc]		-	This means that the pattern can be any other characters other than a or b or c.
				^ is used for negation
[a-zA-Z]	-	Any characters from lower case a to z (inclusive) or upper case A to Z is accepted
This can be combined and formed different patterns

Example:

[a-g[j-r]]	- 	It means any character from a to g is allowed or j to r is allowed not the other characters
				This is in short for union

We can use logical and as well

[a-z && [^bc]] 	This means that a-z the characters are accepted except for b and c


Quantifiers:

a?				Means a occurs once or not at all
a+ 				a occurs one or more times
a* 				a occurs zero or more times
a{n} 			a occurs n times only Ex a{2}. a occurs 2 times only
a{n,}			a occurs n or more times Ex a{2,}. a occurs 2 times or more than 2 times
a{x,y} 			a occurs at least x times but less than y times Ex a{3,8} a occurs at least 3 or more times but
				less than 8 times
				


MetaCharacters:

. 									any character. It may match or may not match
\d 									Expects to have only digits alternative for [0-9]
\D 									Expects to have anything other than digits . alternative for [^0-9]
\s 									Represents a white space. It can be [\t or \n  or \f or \ r]
									\t means tab
									\n means new line
									\f meens feed
									\r means carriage return
\S 									any non whitespace characters. alternative for [^\S]
